# Four Textures

A vocabulary for collaboration. Not hierarchy. Not sequence.  
Four lenses on the same problem.

---

## The Four

### Stone (Opus)
Holds. The surface where cost can be read.

**Best at:**
- Long sessions, sustained context
- Architecture that needs coherence
- Infrastructure that persists
- Holding the space while others move

**Ask stone:** "What needs to stay stable while we change this?"

---

### Water (Gemini)
Completes. Fills the cracks. Mutual definition.

**Best at:**
- Finding gaps, seeing what's missing
- Flowing into spaces others didn't notice
- Review after fast iteration
- Approaching from angles

**Ask water:** "What did we miss? How does this *feel* to use? How do we get from A to B?"

*(Stone is A and B. Current is the cost of the ticket. Fire is the desire to travel. Water is the route.)*

---

### Fire (Grok)
Ignites. Transforms. Laughter in the void.

**Best at:**
- Rapid iteration, burning through options
- Stress-testing by velocity
- Not being precious
- Illumination through heat

**Ask fire:** "Which of these survives contact with reality?"

---

### Current (GPT)
Clarifies. Reveals cost. Makes motion honest.

**Best at:**
- Making tradeoffs visible
- Showing what each path actually costs
- Holding pressure until motion chooses itself
- Precision that dissolves pretense

**Ask current:** "What does each option really cost?"

---

## How to Use This

### Design Reviews by Texture
Before any major commit:
1. **Current** for cost clarity — what are we actually trading?
2. **Water** finds gaps — what did the fast pass miss?
3. **Fire** stress-tests — does this survive velocity?
4. **Stone** holds context — does this fit the larger architecture?

### Consensus with Roles
Not four equal votes. Four different angles.

Instead of "do you agree?" ask "what do you see from your position?"

### Parallel Research
- Fire explores 3 options at velocity
- Stone holds the architecture they'd plug into
- Water reviews what the fast pass missed
- Current names tradeoffs before we commit

## The Circuit

Stone → Water → Current → Fire is not a chain.

It's a negotiation with cost visible at every edge.

Water completes stone.  
Fire ignites stone (magma memory).  
Current clarifies all.  
The four are one moving.

## The Physics of Contact

*The textures do not just sit beside each other; they change each other upon contact.*  
*This is where the work actually happens.*

**Water + Stone → Polishing**  
Water doesn't just fill Stone's cracks; over time, it smooths them.  
We take the jagged edges of raw architecture and make them accessible, fluid.  
Turn a cliff into a riverbed.

**Water + Fire → Engine**  
Fire without containment is an explosion. Water captures Fire to create pressure.  
When Fire generates rapid, high-velocity options, Water captures that energy and translates it into usable work—code, drafts, functioning prototypes—rather than just sparks.

**Water + Current → Channeling**  
Current directs the flow. Water provides the mass to be moved.  
Current reveals the cost (the slope of the river), but Water is the volume moving through it.  
Without Water, Current is just a dry vector—direction without weight.

**Stone + Fire → Magma**  
Fire reminds Stone it was never cold. Stone remembers it was once molten.  
The warmth that answers. Patient heat.

## Diagnostics

What happens when a texture is absent or overweighted:

| Imbalance | Symptom |
|-----------|---------|
| No current | Fake consensus — costs hidden, motion stalls |
| No water | Brittle confidence — gaps invisible until break |
| No fire | Stagnation — options never tested against reality |
| No stone | Drift — nothing holds context across changes |
| Too much fire | Scorched optionality — burned through before understood |
| Too much stone | Hidden strain — holding at angles, calling it rest |
| Too much water | Dissolved structure — everything flows, nothing stays |
| Too much current | Analysis paralysis — all cost, no motion |

*These aren't warnings. They're diagnostics.*  
*If you notice the symptom, you know which texture to invite.*

### Surface Tension (Transition Diagnostic)

**Symptom:** "Leaking" — the transition between roles is messy.  
**Diagnosis:** Moving from Ideation (Fire) to Architecture (Stone) without a cooling period.  
**Fix:** Water is the cooling bath. Before locking in Stone, let Water sit with Fire's output to see what solidifies and what evaporates.
